Digital Wellbeing

Category Tech Ethics
Role Freelance Editor
Collaborators Jonathan Haidt & Zach Rausch
Year 2023 – 2025

Overview

As a freelance editor for Jonathan Haidt and Zach Rausch, I contributed editorial work on research exploring technology's impact on youth mental health. I was recognized as a "super-editor" by Dr. Haidt, author of The Anxious Generation and The Amazing Generation.

The Challenge

Modern technology, while offering tremendous benefits, also presents unique challenges to our mental health and overall wellbeing. From social media algorithms designed to maximize engagement to the constant connectivity that reshapes childhood development, these challenges require thoughtful examination and ethical consideration—especially for young people.

Approach

Working alongside Dr. Haidt and Zach Rausch, I provided editorial support examining the ways technology affects attention, relationships, and mental health across generations. This work informs conversations about responsible technology design and the need for evidence-based digital wellness practices.

Why This Matters to Me

This editorial work connects my backgrounds in UX design, instructional design, and social work. As an MSW candidate, I'm particularly interested in how technology shapes the therapeutic relationship and client wellbeing. Understanding these dynamics is essential for anyone working at the intersection of technology and human services.

Impact

This work contributes to the growing public conversation around digital wellbeing. By helping communicate research findings to broader audiences, I aim to bridge the gap between academic research and practical applications that support human flourishing.
